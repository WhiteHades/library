# Scripture, authority, and the problem of selective reading

## quick read

- The later debate is not only about what the texts say. It is also about who gets to decide which texts speak most loudly.
- Difficult passages are repeatedly absorbed by appeal to context, school, guru, mode, or changing age.
- That does not automatically make the readings false, but it does turn authority itself into part of the problem.

## note

By the second part of the material, the argument has shifted from broad claims to stress tests. The stress tests are the awkward passages: pre-puberty marriage language in a Prabhupada purport, the Mohini-Shiva episode, Rama and Sita eating meat, and horse sacrifice with claims about the animal being elevated through ritual death. None of these are brought in merely for shock. They are brought in to test interpretive consistency.

The same pattern appears again and again. A troubling passage is raised. The reply comes through one or more filters: this was for an earlier age, this applies only in a lower mode, this must be understood through guru lineage, this practice changed, this is not the highest teaching, or this school does not read the text that way. Each filter can be intelligible on its own. The cumulative effect, however, is that scriptural authority starts to feel unstable under pressure.

This is where the debate over Gita and Upanishads becomes important. At one point, the Gita is treated as decisive over apparently lower or earlier strands. At another point, it is still said to be the distilled milk of the Upanishadic cow. That creates a serious note to hold onto: if one text depends on another for authority, yet can also overrule it in practice, then the hierarchy itself needs clearer explanation.

The attached source report helps here because it anchors the debate in actual references. It confirms that the arguments did not arise from thin air. The real question, then, is not whether the texts contain difficult material. They plainly do. The question is whether the interpretive method handling that material remains stable enough to carry a universal truth claim.
